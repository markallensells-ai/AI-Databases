Infrastructure: How the physical AI engine is built.

Data centers are power, real estate, construction, cooling, fiber, and operations projects combined into one massive system.

1. Land + site

Power availability, fiber access, zoning, expansion room, and low disaster risk.

2. Power system

Utility lines, substations, switchgear, UPS batteries, generators, and rack distribution.

3. Cooling system

Air cooling, chilled water, direct-to-chip liquid cooling, containment, and heat rejection.

Security: Protecting the AI brain.

AI infrastructure security has physical security, network security, identity access, monitoring, encryption, backups, and threat detection.

Physical layer

Guards, cameras, fences, biometrics, mantraps, cages, and visitor controls.

Cyber layer

Firewalls, identity management, segmentation, logging, anomaly detection, and encryption.

Recovery layer

Backups, disaster recovery, redundancy, testing, and incident-response planning.

Network: How AI data moves.

Networks connect the data center to carriers, cloud regions, users, companies, and other facilities. Low latency and high bandwidth are critical.

Systems: How all the pieces work together.

The full system is a loop: demand creates compute load, compute creates heat, heat requires cooling, cooling requires power, power requires infrastructure, and everything requires monitoring.

Input

User prompts, applications, enterprise workloads, automation requests, model training data, and stored information.

Processing

Servers, GPUs, databases, networking, scheduling, memory, storage, and AI software layers.

Output

Answers, predictions, recommendations, images, business automation, analytics, and decisions.
